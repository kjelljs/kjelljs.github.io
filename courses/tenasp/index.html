Here is the site for the mini-course "Formal semantics of tense and aspect: A basic introduction" at the Arctic University of Norway (UiT) June 6–8 2018.

It consists of three parts:

  1. Events, aktionsart (telic or anti-telic), aspect ((im)perfective, progressive), times
  2. Times continued, tense, temporal adverbials (particularly positional ones)
  3. The perfect, complex tense, and the SOT (sequence-of-tense) parameter

Selected survey articles

  • Grønn, Atle (2015) On (in)definite tense and aspect in Russian. In Gerhild Zybatow et al. (eds.), Slavic Grammar from a Formal Perspective, Frankfurt: Peter Lang, 175–196.
  • Grønn, Atle and Arnim von Stechow (t.a.) The perfect. To appear in Daniel Gutzmann et al. (eds.) Wiley's Companion to Semantics. 
  • Grønn, Atle and Arnim von Stechow (2016) Tense. In Maria Aloni and Paul Dekker (eds.), Handbook of Formal Semantics, Cambridge: CUP, 313–341.
  • Ogihara, Toshiyuki (2007) Tense and aspect in truth-conditional semantics. Lingua 117: 392–418.
  • Rathert, Monika (2012) Adverbials, in Robert I. Binnick (ed.), The Oxford Handbook of Tense and Aspect, Oxford: OUP.
  • Stechow, Arnim von (2009) Tenses in compositional semantics. In Wolfgang Klein and Ping Li (eds.), The Expression of Time in Language, Berlin: Mouton de Gruyter, 129–166.
